Foreword

I've always found writing to be particularly cathartic, both emotionally and intellectually. Plenty will attest to the former--that writing provides authors with an avenue to sanity. But it's the latter effect I've found more powerful. Like many, I've often found myself drowned in some scientific question, some quantitative conundrum, or some computational task that takes over the mind. In active work and in moments of rest, in times of focus and in dreams, these issues hold a firm, constant grasp on our minds, paradoxially preventing us from working toward resolution.

It's in these times that I turn to writing. There's little attention paid to either the quality or content of what I write. (If you choose to read the pieces below, you may well discover this yourself.) To write well or to write about something is precisely besides the point. The goal is to not think--to allow oneself to be lost in the very thoughts that attempt to lose you. And by submitting to this loss, you find.
